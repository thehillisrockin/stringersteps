Before the workshop

Let's get you ready to build your second brain.

A few quick things to set up first, so on Thursday we go straight to building, no waiting around.

About 15 minutes, one step at a time. You'll check off each step as you go. If you can download an app and answer a few questions, you've got this.

Only 4 required steps. Everything after is optional, and your progress saves automatically.
Required · Step 1 of 4

Use a real computer.

Be on a laptop or desktop, Mac or PC. Not your phone or a tablet, because we're actually building something.

Keep your phone nearby too. We may grab a few things off it.
Required · Step 2 of 4

Where should your brain live?

Your second brain is just a folder. Pick where you want it. Not sure? Most people choose the cloud.

Pick one, then download the desktop app and sign in. We don't need to make a free account.

Required · Step 3 of 4

Make one empty folder.

Create a new folder and name it something like "My Second Brain" (use your own name). Put it wherever you decided in the last step.

Leave it completely empty. We fill it together on Thursday, that's the fun part.
Required · Step 4 of 4

Install Claude on your computer.

This is the one people miss, so let's get it right.

  • aGet a Claude account on at least the $20/month plan ("Pro"). The free version won't work.
  • bDownload the Claude desktop app and sign in (button below).
  • cDo not use Claude in a web browser. It has to be the app.
Using ChatGPT instead? You can do this with Codex. Just make sure you're on a paid ChatGPT plan, then download Codex. We'll be demoing everything in Claude on the call, since that's what Phil uses.

Optional

Grab a few things to feed it.

On your desktop, make a folder called "to-add" and drop in a small handful of things you know you want in your brain.

A few meeting notes, one or two important PDFs, an export of your notes. Five to ten files is plenty.

Everything else gets pulled in automatically later, so you're not missing anything.

Good news

No need to clean anything up.

You don't have to sort, organize, or delete a single thing before Thursday. If you want to tidy something up, go for it, but honestly it's not necessary.

Claude organizes all of it for you. The only real mistake is thinking "I can't start until it's clean," because that's the thing that stops people before they begin. You can start exactly as messy as you are.

Last thing

What Thursday looks like.

Watch Phil's screen, then do it on yours. Building takes time, so there will be quiet stretches where everyone's just working. That's normal.

If you fall behind, don't stress. You get the full replay and a step-by-step PDF, so you can finish at your own pace.
