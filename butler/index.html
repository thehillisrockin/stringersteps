The Easy Clean · Step 1 of 5

Archive, never delete

This is the mindset unlock. Archiving hides an email from your inbox but keeps it searchable forever. Nothing is lost. You can pull it back up in two seconds.

Deleting is scary, so you hesitate. Archiving is free, so you can clear ruthlessly without fear.

From here on, everything in this guide archives. Nothing ever gets deleted.
(2)
The Easy Clean · Step 2 of 5

Your inbox is a to-do list

New rule: the inbox only holds things that need you to act. Everything else gets archived.

If you can search for it later, it doesn't need to sit in front of you.

A newsletter isn't a to-do. A receipt isn't a to-do. A real person waiting on your answer? That's a to-do.
(3)
The Easy Clean · Step 3 of 5

The 10-minute sender sweep

Kill your loudest sender first. In Gmail:

1
Click the search bar at the top. Type from: plus the sender who floods you most. Example: from:newsletter@store.com. Press Enter.
2
Click the checkbox above the email list. That selects everything on the page.
3
A line appears above your emails. Click the blue "Select all conversations that match this search".
4
Click the Archive button, the box with the down arrow. Hundreds gone in one move.
All 50 conversations on this page are selected. Select all conversations that match this search
The highlighted icon is Archive, exactly as Gmail shows it.

Repeat for your top 10 worst senders. One sender at a time, hundreds gone per sweep.

Not on Gmail? Same idea works in Outlook (search the sender, select all, Archive) and Apple Mail.

Teach it

Two little habits make your butler smarter every week:
